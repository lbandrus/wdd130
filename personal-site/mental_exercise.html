Exercises

Look for Mental Distortions

  • A few things to watch for in your thinking patterns. Write the following down when you start to experience them
  • All or nothing Thinking: black and white thinking. If it is not perfect it is a failure
  • Over generalization: Constant thinking of failure.
  • Over focus: Over dwelling on past mistakes.
  • Pushing out the Positive: Positive experiences are not enough.
  • Jumping to conclusions: thinking that others think you are a failure.

Box Breathing

Focus on your breathing. If you are having a panic attack focus on holding your breath for 6 seconds and then breathe out slowly. Repeat the process until the panic attack has passed. This is a good exercise even if you are not having a panic attack. You can slow a racing mind and create healthy thinking patterns.

Create a Worry Tree

Create a worry tree by starting what you are worrying about. Then ask questions to yourself about if you can fix the problem. Then branch off from your answers . For example if you can't do anything about it then let it go. If you can then aske yourself when it comes up, what is it, and the how behind it and branch off until you find solutions to the worry.

Monitor Eating and Sleep Patterns

Diet and sleep can effect your minds in many different ways. Sleep can make you more tired. Being tired you are more likely to make mistakes making you feel less of yourself. Healthy dieting can help give your brain the help it needs to think clearly and make positive decisions. Make sure you are drinking plenty of water and eating plenty of veggies and fruit. Eating excess sugar will slow your brain down and make you feel drowsy.
