Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to help people who are struggling with any form of mental illness to find help. Mental Health is a big issue that needs to be addressed. 1 in 4 people suffer from some kind of mental illness. I want to make a website that connects people to different resources and exercises. People from all walks of life need help with mental needs and it is becoming more of an issue. Having a healthy mind can make all the difference and allow everyone to enjoy life without the mental illness weighing them down. Mental illness can affect our relationships, career, and families. I want to be able to reach out to as many people as possible and help people just like me. I myself have struggled with Anxiety and Depression. I want to share what has helped me and pass it on to other people that are struggling and make the world a better place for all.

Audience

It can be hard to find help when you feel alone. I want to help as many people as I can with mental health because I also have some mental illnesses. I have anxiety and depression. I want people to be able to find the resources that are available. Mental health is important for everyone and any age. It effects men and women. It is also a growing concern.

Here at Mental Ease we want to help everyone to be able to find help and be health in both body and especially mind. Mental Health

Mental anxiety come in many forms. The more come forms are Intense, excessive and persistent worrying and fear over daily experiences. Symptoms include: raised heart rate, constant worrying that is interfering with daily life, shaking, hyperventilation, feeling of being in danger. Treatments include better sleep, healthy dieting, avoiding caffeine and addictive substances. Clinical depression is a constant depressed mood and loss of interest in activities. Depression usually occurs after loss of a loved one or going through a traumatic experience. Some symptoms include constant sadness, insomnia, loss of appetite and thoughts of suicide. Depression is usually best treated through therapies and medication. Meditation can also help with less depression episodes

Mental Exercises

A few things to watch for in your thinking patterns. Write the following down when you start to experience them. All or nothing Thinking: black and white thinking. If it is not perfect it is a failure Over generalization: Constant thinking of failure. Over focus: Over dwelling on past mistakes. Pushing out the Positive: Positive experiences are not enough. Jumping to conclusions: thinking that others think you are a failure. Box Breathing Focus on your breathing. If you are having a panic attack focus on holding your breath for 6 seconds and then breathe out slowly. Repeat the process until the panic attack has passed. This is a good exercise even if you are not having a panic attack. You can slow a racing mind and create healthy thinking patterns. Create a Worry Tree Create a worry tree by starting what you are worrying about. Then ask questions to yourself about if you can fix the problem. Then branch off from your answers . For example if you can't do anything about it then let it go. If you can then aske yourself when it comes up, what is it, and the how behind it and branch off until you find solutions to the worry. Monitor Eating and Sleep Patterns Diet and sleep can effect your minds in many different ways. Sleep can make you more tired. Being tired you are more likely to make mistakes making you feel less of yourself. Healthy dieting can help give your brain the help it needs to think clearly and make positive decisions. Make sure you are drinking plenty of water and eating plenty of veggies and fruit. Eating excess sugar will slow your brain down and make you feel drowsy.
